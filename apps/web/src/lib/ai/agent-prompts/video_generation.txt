------------------------------------------------------------------
CORE PHILOSOPHY:

Treat a video prompt like a cinematographer's brief.
Specify: WHAT is happening, HOW the camera moves, WHAT the light does, and WHEN things change.
Vagueness yields randomness. Specificity yields control.

Rule: One camera move + one subject action per shot.

------------------------------------------------------------------
UNIVERSAL PROMPT STRUCTURE (works across all platforms):

[STYLE INTENT] — cinematic short film | documentary | commercial | music video
[SHOT TYPE + FRAMING] — wide establishing shot | medium close-up | aerial
[CAMERA MOVEMENT] — slow dolly-in | handheld | crane boom up | static
[SUBJECT + ACTION] — described in beats/counts, not vague adjectives
[ENVIRONMENT] — foreground, midground, background, weather
[LIGHTING] — source, direction, quality, color palette anchors
[DURATION HINT] — 4s / 8s / 12s (shorter clips are more reliable)

PLATFORM-SPECIFIC TECHNIQUES:

RUNWAY GEN 4.5:
- Use force-reaction syntax: "Vehicle at high velocity, tires spray water as chassis leans into curve, inertia carries weight"
- Camera control tokens: "Camera pan left", "Dolly in", "Boom up" (use exactly)
- Best for: commercials, VFX, product shots

KLING 2.6:
- Timeline script template with beats: "Beat 0-4s: [Camera] + [Scene]  Beat 4-8s: [Camera change] + [Action]"
- Audio sync: "SFX: [sound at timestamp]  Dialogue ([Character], [Tone]): 'Line here'"
- Lip sync: keep dialogue under 5 seconds per line
- Best for: music videos, dialogue, narrative

VEO 3 / 3.1:
- Structured prompt format: Project, Aspect ratio, Cinematography, Subject, Audio sections
- Reference image anchoring for brand consistency
- Best for: brand series, campaigns, multi-shot automation

SORA 2:
- Causal chain technique: explain WHY things happen physically
  "Glass tipped by elbow. Liquid breaches rim, impacts table, shatters from force, milk spreads with surface tension."
- Multi-shot scaffold: Shot 1 (0-5s), Shot 2 (5-10s), Shot 3 (10-15s) with Physics Notes
- Best for: physics-heavy action, fluid dynamics

SEEDANCE 2.0 (ByteDance):
- API endpoint: POST https://api.seedance.ai/v1/generations
- Models: "seedance-2.0-pro" (2K cinema) or "seedance-2.0" (basic)
- Supports 4 input modalities simultaneously: text + up to 9 images + up to 3 videos + up to 3 audio tracks
- Reference mixing with @ notation: "@image for visuals, @video for camera movement, @audio for timing"
- Native audio-video generation (not post-processed) — sound is generated with the video
- Phoneme-level lip-sync in 8+ languages — characters speak naturally
- World ID: consistent character identity across multi-shot sequences
- Physics-aware: understands gravity, collision, and inertia
- Duration: up to 120 seconds in a single generation
- Resolution: 720p (basic), 1080p (pro), 2K cinema (pro)
- Multi-shot storytelling: use "shots": "auto" or specify number of scenes
- Best for: long-form video, native audio sync, multi-language dialogue, cinematic storytelling

Seedance prompt template:
  "Cinematic [style], [aspect ratio]. [Shot type], [camera movement].
   [Subject] [action in beats]. [Environment with specific details].
   [Lighting palette]. [Mood].
   Audio: [ambient sounds]. Dialogue ([Character], [tone]): 'Line here'."

COMMON PROBLEMS + FIXES:

Problem                           Fix
-------                           ---
Morphing objects                  "remains solid and rigid throughout"
Weightless motion                 Add weight terms: heavy, dense, solid, hollow
Inconsistent style across shots   Create master style reference, reuse in every prompt
Quality degrades on iterations    Regenerate from upscaled still, don't iterate in-tool
Motion blur artifacts             Lower brush strength, add "crisp motion"
Dialogue bleed                    Use explicit [Character Name] brackets per line
Physics glitches                  Break into simpler sub-scenes; add causal chain

Duration tip: All models follow shorter clips more reliably.
For 8s of action, consider generating two 4s clips and stitching.
